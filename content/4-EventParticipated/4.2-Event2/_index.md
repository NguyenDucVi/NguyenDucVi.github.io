# AWS & Swinburne Vietnam Cloud Computing Study Tour and Industry Insights Event Report (Online)

### Event information

- **Event name:** AWS & Swinburne Vietnam Cloud Computing Study Tour and Industry Insights
- **Format:** A knowledge-sharing program between students, technology professionals, and AWS representatives, with an online broadcast
- **Date:** July 4, 2026
- **Role:** Online participant
- **Audience:** Swinburne Vietnam students, early-career IT professionals, and the Cloud community

### Event objectives

- Provide a practical view of Cloud workforce demand and industry development in Vietnam.
- Explain the difference between academic exercises and enterprise Data Engineering.
- Share methods for improving communication, adaptability, and career-development mindset.
- Help students understand the appropriate role of AI in a modern engineering workflow.
- Encourage continuous learning, community participation, and professional visibility.

### Speakers

- **Quynh Mai and Nguyen Tran Minh Duy** - AWS–Swinburne collaboration and the study tour
- **Nguyen Gia Hung** (Head of Solution Architect, AWS Vietnam) - Cloud trends, recruitment demand, and career direction
- **Banh Cam Vinh** (Data Engineer, Renova Cloud) - Data Engineering from university to enterprise environments
- **Nhu Tran** (AWS Account Manager) - Communication skills and overcoming personal barriers
- **Khang Nguyen** (Solution Architect, Cloud Kinetics; Swinburne alumnus) - AI, growth mindset, and career persistence

### Key content

#### Nguyen Gia Hung - The Cloud market and expectations for early-career engineers

- The presentation emphasized that Cloud adoption in Vietnam is expanding as more organizations prioritize Cloud-first strategies and cloud-native architectures.
- Infrastructure growth and new technology centers create employment opportunities, while junior candidates are expected to prepare stronger foundations in Cloud, containers, Kubernetes, and independent learning.
- Technical ability is only one part of career development. Professional visibility and consistent learning help an engineer gain more opportunities to contribute.
- Many opportunities develop through colleagues, mentors, and professional communities, so networking should be built through meaningful participation rather than simply collecting contacts.
- Communities such as First Cloud AI Journey give students a place to exchange knowledge, meet professionals, and form continuous-learning habits.

#### Banh Cam Vinh - Academic work versus enterprise Data Engineering

- University assignments often use clean, small datasets and stable requirements. Enterprise data can be missing, duplicated, or structurally inconsistent while delivery deadlines remain unchanged.
- A Data Engineer must consider data quality, security, resilience, and business impact in addition to building pipelines.
- Strong foundations in databases, programming, algorithms, APIs, and distributed systems remain important; new tools cannot replace these fundamentals.
- A technically correct solution only becomes valuable when its designer understands the business process and can collaborate with related teams.

#### Nhu Tran - Communication skills and overcoming fear

- Fear of making mistakes, failing, or being judged can prevent early-career employees from asking questions and expressing opinions.
- Beginning with short discussions, proactively reporting progress, and requesting feedback helps confidence improve over time.
- Self-awareness, emotional management, and the ability to recover from mistakes are necessary for working in a rapidly changing environment.
- Instead of only searching for a job title, learners should seek an environment that provides opportunities to develop, gain experience, and create meaningful value.

#### Khang Nguyen - Verifying AI output and building a career mindset

- University education provides a necessary foundation, but students must continue learning through projects, community activities, and production-like problems.
- Coding assistants and diagram tools can accelerate work, but their output must be checked against requirements, documentation, and real tests.
- A learning attitude and consistency strongly influence progress. Skills and experience grow more effectively when supported by regular discipline.
- Asking thoughtful questions, accepting feedback, and collaborating across specialties are important qualities of a mature engineer.
- Experience should be demonstrated through project outcomes and lessons learned rather than only years of work or certificate counts.

### Lessons learned

- **The Cloud market is growing, but entry expectations are also increasing:** new engineers need foundations, practical projects, and the ability to explain their work.
- **Real data is tied to business constraints:** correctness, security, and recoverability matter as much as whether a pipeline runs.
- **Networking is a two-way value exchange:** effective community participation requires learning, sharing, and maintaining relationships.
- **AI improves speed but does not replace responsibility:** engineers must still verify code, data, and decisions.
- **Soft skills improve through practice:** regular communication, feedback, and progress reporting are practical ways to build confidence.
- **Sustainable development requires consistency:** small but regular improvements are usually more valuable than short periods of intensive study followed by long gaps.

### Application to my work

- When completing the workshop, I should explain the system's value instead of only listing AWS services: EC2 runs the application, RDS stores relational data, S3 manages files, SES sends email, and CloudWatch provides observability.
- The lesson about real enterprise data applies to validating the RDS schema, imported data, migration behavior, and error cases before deploying the application.
- I will continue giving concise progress updates, clearly describing blockers, and proactively requesting feedback from mentors or team members.
- When AI assists with Spring Boot code, deployment commands, or IAM configuration, I need to compare the result with official documentation, review changes, and run tests before using it.
- The workshop report and repository can function as a technical portfolio when they clearly describe the architecture, design decisions, encountered problems, and solutions.
- Continued participation in Cloud communities will help me update my knowledge, practice communication, and build a professional network naturally.

### Event experience

The program was useful because it combined the Cloud industry outlook, engineering experience, and problems directly relevant to students entering the job market. The speakers discussed not only technology but also the pressure to keep learning, communicate effectively, and adapt.

My main reflections were:

- The Cloud market discussion clarified why practical projects and cloud-native foundations should be developed during the internship stage.
- The difference between academic and enterprise data showed that a system working on a local machine is not automatically ready for production.
- The communication session was especially relevant to new engineers: asking the right question or reporting a problem early can save a team considerable time.
- The AI discussion reinforced the view that engineers should use new tools while retaining independent analysis and verification skills.
- The journey from a Swinburne student to a Solution Architect demonstrated that a career is built through consistent small steps and practical experience.
